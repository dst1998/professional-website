    Floating-Point Compute-in-Memory Engine with RRAM-eDRAM Fusion

    <p class="desc mb-2">Research Assistant, NanoX Lab, Purdue University (Aug 2023–present).</p>
  
  
<div><ul>
  • Developed a floating-point compute-in-memory engine with RRAM-eDRAM fusion macros and multiply-accumulate-compute dataflow for high-speed, energy-efficient edge AI acceleration.
  • Implemented a pipelined accumulator and power-aware synthesis with a multi-Vt library, decoupled gain-cell eDRAM read/write ports, and doubled effective bandwidth through interleaved macro access.
  • Achieved 38.5 TFLOPS/W energy efficiency at 600 MHz, 1.75% Tiny-ViT accuracy degradation on CIFAR-10, and up to 4.84x/8.48x figure-of-merit gains over prior SRAM/RRAM baselines.

    • </ul> </div>
